# 📊 Top 1000 IMDb Movies – Exploratory Data Analysis



Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) of the 1,000 most popular movies on IMDb between 2006 and 2016



> 📖 \*\*Languages:\*\* \*\*English\*\* | \[Deutsch](README.md) | \[Türkçe](README\_TR.md)



## 📋 About the Project



Comprehensive movie analysis of the 1,000 most popular IMDb movies with a focus on:



* Rating structure and concentration analysis in the elite segment
* Relationship between popularity (votes) and ratings
* Temporal distribution and yearly clustering of movies
* Genre distribution and dominant genre combinations
* Actor analysis and star-power effect



## 📦 Dataset



**Data Source:** [IMDb Top 1000 Movies (2006–2016) - Kaggle](https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/PromptCloudHQ/imdb-data)



**1,000 observations** with 12 variables per movie



**Analysis areas:** Rating distribution, genre frequency, actor analysis, yearly trends, revenue correlations



## 🔍 Key Findings



✅ It was observed that the ratings of movies in the elite segment are concentrated in a narrow range and the overall rating level is high.  
✅ No strong linear relationship was found between the number of votes and ratings, suggesting that high popularity does not always mean higher ratings.  
✅ The distribution of movies by year shows concentrations in certain periods, with the elite segment appearing in a cluster-like pattern in specific years.  
✅ The genre distribution analysis shows that combinations of Action, Adventure and Drama dominate the elite segment.  
✅ The actor analysis shows that certain actors repeatedly stand out in the elite segment and a star-power effect is present.
